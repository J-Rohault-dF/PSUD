The United States of America is a federal republic consisting of 50 states, 1 district and 5 territories. It was established in 1776 by taking its independence from the British Empire.

Canada is a constitutional monarchy in the commonwealth, consisting of 10 provinces and 3 territories. It was established in 1867 by taking its independence from the British Empire.

The European Union is a non-sovereign union, consisting of 28 member states. It was established in 1993 by replacing the European Economic Community (established in 1958).

United States Congress

Head of government
President
Elected with a legislative electoral college for a term of 4 years (renewable once)
Upper chamber
Senate
100 members (2 per state) elected using FPTP.
Term is 6 years, with a third of the seats renewed every 2 years.
Lower chamber
House of Representatives
453 members (by states according to population) elected using FPTP by constituency.
All seats are renewed every 2 years.

Parliament of Canada

Head of government
Prime Minister
Elected by the legislature
Upper chamber
Senate
105 members appointed by the governor general.
Legislative power is very low.
Lower chamber
House of Commons
338 members (by provinces and territories according to population) elected using FPTP by electoral riding.
All seats are renewed at once, for a term of at most 5 years.
